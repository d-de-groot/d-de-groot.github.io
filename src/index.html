An Electrifying Story

How does a tree become a self-powered data centre? We wanted to connect the campus to its living environment without leaving a carbon footprint. Our team designed a weather-resistant kinetic generator that hooks up safely to a tree, capturing the mechanical energy of swaying branches to power a network of sensors. This setup fits within the tree's natural footprint, causing zero permanent damage to the tree and the wildlife around it.

Each ElectriciTree doubles as a student hangout spot or workspace featuring a low-energy E-ink display which displays that specific tree's vitals. Scanning the QR code instantly pulls up this website which tracks our collective campus network, allowing us to view our combined environmental impact or toggle between different metrics to see how our campus ecosystem is thriving as a whole.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the system powered if there is no wind to sway the branches?

expand_more

The kinetic energy harvested during windy periods is stored in an integrated, long-life battery backup system. This ensures the eco-sensors and live data streams remain fully operational 24/7, even on completely calm days.

What is the purpose of collecting all this campus environmental data?

expand_more

By collecting the environmental data from all campus trees into this central website, it acts as an open-source campus resource. The data helps track microclimate shifts, monitors local biodiversity health and raise awareness about sustainability by making invisible ecological processes visible. Students and researchers can also use the data for academic projects, sustainability tracking, or just to check which part of campus has the best weather right now.

Is the mechanism built with sustainable materials that are safe to use around trees?

expand_more

Yes, the system specifically uses bio-based Dyneema rope for the mechanism and carbon-negative materials like bio-based glulam for the furniture. This ensures that the components interacting closely with the trees are naturally sourced, minimally disruptive to the environment and free of toxic environmental risks.
